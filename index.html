Scrum Method in Software Project Management

Introduction to Scrum

Scrum is an Agile framework used to manage software development through iterative development cycles called sprints. It emphasizes collaboration, adaptability, and continuous improvement.

Scrum Roles

  • Product Owner: Defines project requirements and priorities.
  • Scrum Master: Ensures Scrum practices are followed and removes obstacles.
  • Development Team: Builds and tests the product increment.

Sprint Structure Used in My Project

During SDLC Stage 4, weekly sprints were used to organize development tasks. Each sprint included planning, task execution, and review of completed work.

Reflection

Scrum allowed structured progress and improved collaboration. Breaking work into sprints reduced risk and allowed adjustments based on feedback.
